# Applied AI Community Leader

*The person who brings applied AI to their city, campus, or community. Not by lecturing about it. By creating the rooms where people experience it for the first time.*

> **This role rewards initiative, people skills, and local knowledge over technical depth.** You do not need to be an engineer. You need to be the person who knows where to find a venue, how to get 30 people in a room, and how to make strangers feel welcome enough to admit they do not know what an agent is. If you are the person people call when they want to know what is happening in your city, this is your role.

---

## What They Do

The Chapter Leader is the local operator of the Applied AI Society. They run events, build partnerships, grow community, and create the conditions for people to have their first real encounter with applied AI.

Three core functions:

**1. Run events.** [Applied AI Live](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/applied-ai-live) is the flagship format: practitioners sharing how they actually make money with applied AI. But there are many formats: [hackathons](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/running-a-hackathon), [live architecture sessions](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/live-architecture-session), [MVJ workshops](/docs/playbooks/practitioner/training-the-mvj-workshop), and more. The Chapter Leader picks the format that fits their community and runs it. Consistently. The value compounds with repetition. One event is a meetup. A recurring event is a movement.

**2. Build the local network.** The Chapter Leader knows who the builders, founders, and operators are in their area. They connect people who should know each other. They find [venues](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/finding-a-venue), [speakers](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/speaker-outreach), [photographers](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/finding-a-photographer), and [partners](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/building-partnerships). They are the connective tissue between the global AAS mission and the local community.

**3. Create encounters.** The most important thing a Chapter Leader does is put people in the room where they see applied AI done well for the first time. Not a demo. Not a pitch deck. A real practitioner solving a real problem. That [encounter](/docs/concepts/the-encounter) is what converts skeptics into practitioners. You cannot create that encounter with content alone. It requires a physical room and a trusted host.

---

## Why This Role Is Emerging Now

Most people's exposure to AI is ChatGPT. They type a question, get an answer, and think "that's cool, I guess." The gap between that and a working [Personal Jarvis](/docs/concepts/personal-jarvis) is enormous. Content alone cannot close it. People need to see it done by someone they relate to, in a room they trust, in their own city.

The Chapter Leader is the person who creates that room. Every city needs one. Every campus needs one. The [Applied AI Society](https://docs.appliedaisociety.org) is a [signalmaxxing](/docs/concepts/signalmaxxing) community comprised of signalmaxxing squads around the world. Each chapter is a squad. The Chapter Leader is the one who forms it.

## The Progression

**Level 1: First Event.** You run your first Applied AI Live or MVJ workshop. You get 10-30 people in a room. You learn what works and what does not. You write a [recap](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/writing-and-sharing-event-recaps).

**Level 2: Recurring Events.** You establish a monthly cadence. You have a venue partner. Speakers are reaching out to you instead of the other way around. The community knows your name.

**Level 3: Local Ecosystem.** You have partnerships with co-working spaces, universities, local companies. You are running multiple event formats. You have a [CRM](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/crm-setup) tracking your community. Other people are helping you run events.

**Level 4: Movement Builder.** Your chapter is self-sustaining. You are training other Chapter Leaders in nearby cities or campuses. You are shaping the culture of applied AI in your region. The local press and institutions know AAS because of you.

---

## Who Is This Role For

- **Community organizers** who already run events or meetups and want to add applied AI to their portfolio
- **College students** who want to bring AI literacy to their campus and surrounding community (see Brian DeSousa's model: a freshman running AI literacy programs at libraries and high schools)
- **Founders and operators** who want to build their local network while contributing to something bigger
- **Anyone who looks around their city and thinks "there should be a place for people figuring out applied AI"**

The prerequisite is not technical skill. It is initiative. You need to be willing to book a venue, invite speakers, promote an event, and show up even when only 8 people come. The playbooks handle the how. You bring the will.

## Getting Started

1. **Read the [Chapter Leader Playbooks](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader).** Start with [Starting a Chapter](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/starting-a-chapter) and [Event Formats](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/event-formats).
2. **Join the community.** Drop into the [Discord](https://discord.gg/K7uWJBMFaN) and introduce yourself.
3. **Pick a date and a venue.** Do not overthink this. The first event does not need to be perfect. It needs to happen.
4. **Run it.** Follow the [Applied AI Live](/docs/playbooks/chapter-leader/applied-ai-live) checklist for your first event.
5. **Write the recap.** Share what happened with the community.
